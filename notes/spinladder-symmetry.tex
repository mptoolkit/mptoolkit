\title{Dynamical $SO(4)$ symmetry in spin ladders}

\author{I. P. McCulloch}
\date{\today}

\maketitle

Consider the spin-$S$ ladder, described by the Hamiltonian
\beq
\begin{array}{rcl}
H &=& \displaystyle J_t \sum_{i=1}^L \vektor{S}_1(i) \cdot
\vektor{S}_2(i) \vspace{1mm} \\
& & + \displaystyle J_l \sum_{<ij>,a} \vektor{S}_a(i) \cdot
\vektor{S}_a(j) \vspace{1mm}  \\
& & + \displaystyle J_{1c} \sum_{i=1}^{L-1} \vektor{S}_1(i) \cdot
\vektor{S}_2(i+1) \vspace{1mm}  \\
& & + \displaystyle J_{2c} \sum_{i=1}^{L-1} \vektor{S}_2(i) \cdot
\vektor{S}_1(i+1)
\end{array}
\eeq
where $\vektor{S}_a(i)$ refers to the spin in ladder $a=1,2$ and $i$
is the site index. We add the constraint that the diagonal couplings
are identical, $J_{1c} = J_{2c} = J_c$.

Now, switch to a variables
\beq
\begin{array}{rcl}
\vektor{Q}(i) &=& \vektor{S}_1(i) + \vektor{S}_2(i) \\
\vektor{R}(i) &=& \vektor{S}_1(i) - \vektor{S}_2{i}
\end{array}
\eeq
